Eminently worthy
Thursday, October 10, 2013

Thanksgiving is nearly upon us, and we’ve got a few suggestions for sprucing up your recipes this year…slip some Herbes de Provence olive oil under the skin of your turkey, splash Cranberry Pear balsamic into your cranberry sauce and mix some Wild Mushroom & Sage olive oil into your stuffing. With Garlic mashed potatoes and a big green salad, you’re well on your way to an Evoolution-inspired Thanksgiving.

But don’t think we forgot about dessert! There’s been talk of Vermont Maple pecan tarts and Honey Ginger pumpkin pie, but imagine our surprise when last week, in an attempt to save some teeny-tiny market pears from the compost bin (yes, we’re still buying too much fruit), we wound up with a pear sauce eminently worthy of our Thanksgiving table.

It’s the simplest thing, really, the kind of sauce you can make half-asleep on Sunday morning (which is always handy). Take the best pears you can find, then peel, core and quarter them. We used little Seckel pears from the City Market, which have firm flesh and exceptional sweetness, and we recommend you do the same. Tumble them into a pot with some sugar, a pinch of salt and a trickling of Cinnamon Pear balsamic. Cook for a long time over low-ish heat, stirring occasionally, but mostly letting them do their thing while you inhale the fragrance of caramelizing sugar.

After a couple of hours, you have Caramelized Pear Sauce – the more complex, elegant cousin of plain-Jane applesauce – to be spooned over ice cream, layered into trifle or eaten with plain whole-milk yogurt for a swoon-worthy breakfast.  This weekend, we’ll be serving a dollop alongside buttery buckwheat cake and salted caramel whipped cream, and a happy Thanksgiving it shall be.

Picnic season
Monday, July 01, 2013

Another long weekend has come and gone, and after a wonderful Canada Day, the workweek may look less appealing than ever. The thing about summer in Edmonton, though, is that every warm, sunny day feels fortuitous. As far as we’re concerned, July and August are like one big, long weekend! We put aside our work for a little while each day and head outdoors, soaking in as much sun as we can.

To take advantage of the clear skies, we’ve been picnicking a lot lately – whether it’s during the lunch hour or after a long walk in the evening, there’s nothing like sitting cross-legged in the grass with good friends and good food. Our favourite meals are the spontaneous ones, such as this, where quality ingredients are thrown together and everyone can nibble away to their heart’s content. A nice salad, with some bread and fruit and cheese, is all you need – provided there is something cold and sparkling to be imbibed alongside.

This grainy salad lends itself to endless variations, with a base of wheatberries and quinoa spruced up with Rainier cherries, arugula, pistachios and pickled radishes. Often, we find ourselves with a little bit of this and that left toward the end of the week – a handful of strawberries, half a bag of greens, etc. – so this is a great way to clear out the fridge before the next market day, and it’s delicious to boot! The first of many...
Sunday, May 26, 2013

Our craving for crunchy summertime salads has been ushered in with the hot, sunny days we’ve been having (barring this weekend!).  Bright and refreshing coleslaw is always a favourite, and we made our first slaw of the season last week, as part of a casual dinner with friends. It's a sweet and spicy slaw, dead simple and full of flavour - the first of many, we think.

Half a small head each of green and red cabbage, a couple of carrots and some radishes were all sliced thinly. Some minced onion was soaked in about ¼ cup of Blenheim Apricot Balsamic (to tame its raw bite), then tossed in with the vegetables. The whole thing was anointed with about ½ cup of Harissa Olive Oil and some salt, and tossed well. Some chopped cilantro on top would have been the kicker!

You might need more or less dressing, depending on the size of your cabbages. Top fish tacos with it, serve with fried chicken, or simply plunk it on the table at your next barbecue and watch it disappear!


Tis the season...
Wednesday, May 15, 2013

The City Market returns to the 4th street promenade this Saturday! Luckily, the debut of the outdoor market coincides with asparagus season. We wait patiently for spring asparagus all year, so we plan to bring a bunch (or two) home with us each week until they’re gone. You should do the same!

Here’s a quick, simple and delicious way to get your fill of our favourite spring vegetable:

Wash and dry asparagus, and trim the ends if needed – we find that most specimens at the market are pre-trimmed and perfectly tender, so you may not need to do this. Line the stalks on a rimmed baking sheet and liberally coat them with oil. We find a rich, nutty oil like our Roasted French Walnut Oil or Roasted Almond Oil is ideal.

Sprinkle with salt and roast at 400 degrees for about 10 minutes, until vibrant green and lightly caramelized; this depends on the size of your asparagus, so use your judgment.

Remove them from the oven and douse with Honey Ginger Balsamic, lemon zest and toasted nuts or seeds – we used mustard seeds this time. You can serve it as a side dish, but more often than not, we just call it dinner. Enjoy!



Iron Chef: Evoolution Edition, Round One
Sunday, March 24, 2013
“What can I do with this?” is one of the most common questions we get from our customers; of course, everyone knows that our oils and vinegars make a delicious salad dressing or marinade, but here at Evoolution, we’re always striving to discover new uses for our products.

To that end, Curtis and Rachel W. decided to make things interesting at our last staff meeting with a little friendly competition. After each of us drew an oil and a vinegar from a basket, we were charged with creating a dish using the combination. Needless to say, some of us were luckier than others, but the wheels were turning in everyone’s heads regardless.

Finally, on one evening last week, it was showtime!

Mark was fortunate enough to be nominated as the judge:



We started with dessert first…

Azkaa made Eureka Lemon ice cream with a Serrano Honey reduction.



Rachel P. made a Wild Blueberry Balsamic crumble with a Persian Lime topping and burnt-honey mascarpone cream.



EJ made seared rare tuna with a Pomegranate, ginger and soy reduction, served with Roasted French Walnut edamame purée, wasabi cream and spiced, candied peanuts.



Rachel W. made Tuscan Herb crostini topped with Vermont Maple Balsamic onion jam, beef tenderloin and horseradish cream.



Curtis made BBQ Ribs with Organic12-Year Balsamic, Garlic EVOO and Applewood Smoked Salt.



After much tasting, and with careful attention to his criteria, Mark named the winning dish: EJ’s tuna! EJ will be the judge for the next competition, and the rest of us have a couple of months to work with our new combinations before the big showdown.
