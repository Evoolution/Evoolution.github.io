Olive Oil Body Scrub
Tuesday, October 15, 2013

Well, Thanksgiving has come and gone! We hope yours was splendid and full of good food. We thought we’d take a breather from the recipes this week (though feel free to check out the new additions to our recipe section) and offer you a few other tips on using our oils.  Our range of Olivella products is much-loved among the staff and customers alike; what you may not know is that in addition to these products, many of us also use pure extra-virgin olive oil in our skin/haircare regimes. Here are a few ways to do the same:

  •  comb a small amount of Blood Orange oil through your hair to keep it smooth and shiny (this is especially helpful for curly hair)
  • dab some olive oil onto a cotton swab and remove your makeup
  • keep a bottle of olive oil next to your sink; after washing dishes, rub a small amount into your hands to moisturize
  • ditch the lip balm and smooth some olive oil onto your lips instead
  • mix one of our varietal olive oils in a 2:1 ratio with castor oil and try out the popular oil-cleansing method


Our personal favourite is a super-simple, inexpensive olive oil body scrub. Combine ½ cup brown sugar with 2 tbsp. olive oil and ¼ cup baking soda. Apply a small amount to wet skin, scrub very gently and rinse away! It leaves your skin incredibly soft and smooth and can be stored neatly in a jar in your shower. We love using our Eureka Lemon oil for a fresh scent! 


Newest Additions
Tuesday, June 25, 2013

In recent weeks, we’ve added a few new items to our selection that we’re excited to tell you about! All of our Northern Hemisphere oils are now in, the newest being an Organic Cerasuola from Sicily.

We've also added a new Yellow Grapefruit Olive Oil, perfect for summer. Ripe yellow grapefruit is crushed with Tunisian olives to deliver a zesty oil. We’ll have a few more new fused oils to show off next week, so (spice lovers) stay tuned!


Iron Chef: Evoolution Edition - Round Two
Friday, June 07, 2013

Last night, we had our second round of Evoolution Iron Chef, with EJ (winner of the previous round) judging as the rest of us threw down! It was a rousing success, and with the Family Fued-esque theme of ‘Things you would find at a BBQ”, everyone brought their A-game. 


Mark made sandwiches with root beer-pulled pork, Alfoos Mango balsamic barbecue sauce, and Herbes de Provence and Alfoos Mango cabbage slaw. 



Rachel W. made Wild Mushroom & Sage rösti with prosciutto and Italian Aromatic salt, topped with portobello mushroom and Blackberry Ginger pork tenderloin. 



Teagan made potato salad with Thai Lemongrass Mint and Porcini mayonnaise, pickles, paprika and boiled eggs.


Rachel P. made sandwiches with Harissa mayonnaise, garlic and scallion pork meatballs and Blenheim Apricot-pickled carrot and radish slaw.



Curtis made stuffed peppers with homemade tomato sauce, garlic, Italian seasoning, rice and Basil olive oil, topped with parmesan cheese and an 18-Year Traditional reduction.



Azkaa toasted challah from Dauphine with Butter olive oil and Strawberry balsamic, then topped it with crushed strawberry-mascarpone cream, fresh strawberries, a dusting of icing sugar and sprigs of fresh thyme. 



After plating and pictures, we all dug in, EJ focusing on the task at hand while the rest of us chatted away. Most of us came hungry in preparation, so by the time we cleared the plates, EJ was ready to announce Rachel P. (a.k.a. me, behind the scenes in the above photo) as the winner. 

As I tend to do the blogging on these nights, we've decided that the lovely runner-up, Azkaa, will be the judge for the next round. Prepare for "Pickles, Jams, Chutneys and Relishes"! 

The first of many...
Sunday, May 26, 2013

Our craving for crunchy summertime salads has been ushered in with the hot, sunny days we’ve been having (barring this weekend!).  Bright and refreshing coleslaw is always a favourite, and we made our first slaw of the season last week, as part of a casual dinner with friends. It's a sweet and spicy slaw, dead simple and full of flavour - the first of many, we think.

Half a small head each of green and red cabbage, a couple of carrots and some radishes were all sliced thinly. Some minced onion was soaked in about ¼ cup of Blenheim Apricot Balsamic (to tame its raw bite), then tossed in with the vegetables. The whole thing was anointed with about ½ cup of Harissa Olive Oil and some salt, and tossed well. Some chopped cilantro on top would have been the kicker!

You might need more or less dressing, depending on the size of your cabbages. Top fish tacos with it, serve with fried chicken, or simply plunk it on the table at your next barbecue and watch it disappear!


Entertaining Made Easy
Wednesday, April 24, 2013

We’re only weeks away from having the City Market on our doorstep again, and we’re already dreaming of how to use our weekly bounty. During market season on the promenade, many of our customers come in asking for suggestions on what to pair with the fruit, vegetables, meat and cheese they’ve just bought. While our oils and vinegars are great for cooking, nothing says summer to us like a leisurely, no-cook meal.

Enter: the cheese board! A few key stops at the market and a bit of tasting at Evoolution is all you need - with a green salad and some fresh bread on the side, dinner is served. In anticipation of warm summer nights to come, we put together one of our own the other night.

Pictured are three cheeses - a nutty sheep’s milk, a nice Caerphilly and some griddled halloumi topped with chestnut honey -, plus a bit of charcuterie, thin slices of pear and sweet ripe figs drizzled with our Blood Orange Olive Oil. To round out the board, we chose a rich Mission Fig Balsamic reduction, Dijon mustard, quince paste and of course, more blood orange oil! A smattering of slivered almonds and toasted whole hazelnuts finished everything off.

With a simple asparagus and fennel salad on the side, it was a perfect prelude to summer. As the weather grows warmer, juicy tomatoes with Basil Olive Oil, fragrant Saskatoon berries with Lavender Balsamic and fresh goat’s cheese with Harissa Olive Oil will surely make an appearance.

All that’s left to do is throw open the windows (or stroll onto the patio) and invite some friends over!
