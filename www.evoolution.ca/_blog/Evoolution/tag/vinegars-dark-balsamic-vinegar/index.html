Eminently worthy
Thursday, October 10, 2013

Thanksgiving is nearly upon us, and we’ve got a few suggestions for sprucing up your recipes this year…slip some Herbes de Provence olive oil under the skin of your turkey, splash Cranberry Pear balsamic into your cranberry sauce and mix some Wild Mushroom & Sage olive oil into your stuffing. With Garlic mashed potatoes and a big green salad, you’re well on your way to an Evoolution-inspired Thanksgiving.

But don’t think we forgot about dessert! There’s been talk of Vermont Maple pecan tarts and Honey Ginger pumpkin pie, but imagine our surprise when last week, in an attempt to save some teeny-tiny market pears from the compost bin (yes, we’re still buying too much fruit), we wound up with a pear sauce eminently worthy of our Thanksgiving table.

It’s the simplest thing, really, the kind of sauce you can make half-asleep on Sunday morning (which is always handy). Take the best pears you can find, then peel, core and quarter them. We used little Seckel pears from the City Market, which have firm flesh and exceptional sweetness, and we recommend you do the same. Tumble them into a pot with some sugar, a pinch of salt and a trickling of Cinnamon Pear balsamic. Cook for a long time over low-ish heat, stirring occasionally, but mostly letting them do their thing while you inhale the fragrance of caramelizing sugar.

After a couple of hours, you have Caramelized Pear Sauce – the more complex, elegant cousin of plain-Jane applesauce – to be spooned over ice cream, layered into trifle or eaten with plain whole-milk yogurt for a swoon-worthy breakfast.  This weekend, we’ll be serving a dollop alongside buttery buckwheat cake and salted caramel whipped cream, and a happy Thanksgiving it shall be.

We’re going to show you one of our favourite pick-me-ups for a rainy day (or a string of them), a very simple but delicious use for our dark balsamic vinegars.

You’ll be familiar with making a reduction by now, of course, but just in case – pour one cup of our dark balsamic vinegar into your smallest saucepan, turn the heat to low and let it slowly, gently bubble away until thick and syrupy; it should have reduced by about half.

We used Espresso balsamic, but Cinnamon Pear or Dark Chocolate would be equally good. If you churn your own ice cream, drizzle the reduction in just before it’s done for a balsamic-rippled treat.

Getting our fill
Thursday, September 05, 2013

We’ve been getting our fill of tomatoes in the past couple of weeks, snatching them up by the armful at the market. They’re at their peak this time of year, ruby-red and juicy, so until the leaves start to change colour, we’re following a largely tomato-based diet. It’s our consolation for summer’s end, and a most effective one at that.

We’ll admit to having eaten tomatoes dressed with olive oil and balsamic as a meal on several occasions, but for those days when we feel like we need something a little more substantial, a simple toasted tomato sandwich does the trick. We toss chopped tomatoes with premium extra-virgin olive oil, a few drops of 18-Year Traditional balsamic vinegar, some slivered basil and some salt, then pile it onto a slice of garlic-rubbed toast. A second slice of toast, set on top with a hearty smash, is optional – a napkin (trust us!) is not.

Now, the above is a perfectly delicious lunch for one or two. But if you have company, especially for dinner, it’s nice to gild the lily a bit. In this case, we strongly suggest you set your oven to 250 degrees and halve some tomatoes, fitting them cut-side-up in a single layer into a casserole dish (or high-rimmed baking sheet). Pour some premium extra-virgin olive oil over, enough to coat the tomatoes and cover the bottom of the dish completely (this is usually about a cup of oil – the tomatoes do not need to be submerged). Sprinkle on some salt and pepper, and send them into the oven for 2.5 hours, flipping them after the first hour and again after the second, so they emerge as they entered, cut-side-up.

They will be burnished and crinkled on the outside, silky and tender within, and will taste like the ripest, sweetest tomatoes imaginable. While still warm, add some sliced garlic, scallions and basil and let cool to room temperature.

Serve the halved tomatoes with toasted bread and goat’s cheese, or chop them and use as a pasta sauce. Sometimes, we just put a spoonful or two on the side of our plate, as a condiment. Store them in a jar in the fridge, covered in the tomato-flavoured oil – you will have some of this wonderfully fragrant oil left over, so keep in mind that it is good for frying eggs, saucing pasta, dipping bread, drizzling over fish or roasting potatoes. Enjoy.

Entertaining Made Easy
Wednesday, April 24, 2013

We’re only weeks away from having the City Market on our doorstep again, and we’re already dreaming of how to use our weekly bounty. During market season on the promenade, many of our customers come in asking for suggestions on what to pair with the fruit, vegetables, meat and cheese they’ve just bought. While our oils and vinegars are great for cooking, nothing says summer to us like a leisurely, no-cook meal.

Enter: the cheese board! A few key stops at the market and a bit of tasting at Evoolution is all you need - with a green salad and some fresh bread on the side, dinner is served. In anticipation of warm summer nights to come, we put together one of our own the other night.

Pictured are three cheeses - a nutty sheep’s milk, a nice Caerphilly and some griddled halloumi topped with chestnut honey -, plus a bit of charcuterie, thin slices of pear and sweet ripe figs drizzled with our Blood Orange Olive Oil. To round out the board, we chose a rich Mission Fig Balsamic reduction, Dijon mustard, quince paste and of course, more blood orange oil! A smattering of slivered almonds and toasted whole hazelnuts finished everything off.

With a simple asparagus and fennel salad on the side, it was a perfect prelude to summer. As the weather grows warmer, juicy tomatoes with Basil Olive Oil, fragrant Saskatoon berries with Lavender Balsamic and fresh goat’s cheese with Harissa Olive Oil will surely make an appearance.

All that’s left to do is throw open the windows (or stroll onto the patio) and invite some friends over! 


Just in time for Spring...
Friday, April 12, 2013

As the snow starts to melt and sunshine streams into the store, visions of fruit and flowers fill our heads here at Evoolution. Luckily, we have received two new balsamic vinegars that are perfect for Spring.


Our Lavender dark balsamic is intensely fragrant with a floral sweetness and slightly herbal notes. For dessert lovers, try including it in chocolate ganache, whipped cream, shortbread or sorbet. On the savoury side, it would make a fantastic potato salad with our Herbes de Provence EVOO – perfect for a spring picnic!



Our Coconut white balsamic is rich and toasty, with the unmistakable flavour of sweet coconut. Combined with our Persian Lime or Garlic EVOO, it would make a lovely marinade for seafood or stir-fried vegetables. For breakfast, we would reduce it and pour over pancakes, or spread it like honey onto buttered toast.

Be sure to come by the store and savour a little taste of Spring with us!
