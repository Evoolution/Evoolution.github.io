Iron Chef: Evoolution Edition - Round Five
Saturday, May 31, 2014

We had the latest instalment of Iron Chef: Evoolution a little while ago, and it was the sweetest one yet! The theme was "Gluten-Free Desserts", and everybody rose to the challenge admirably.


Darren made ginger scones with Blackberry-Ginger mole reduction


Colin made cinnamon buns with Red Apple-maple syrup reduction


EJ made Alfoos Mango-coconut cassava cake with coconut custard


Azkaa made Eureka Lemon almond muffins with lavender glaze and local florae (and home-ground flour!)


Curtis made Blood Orange chocolate truffles

This was the first challenge in which Rachel W, our judge and resident gluten-free'er, was able to taste all of the dishes. Everyone commenced getting a sugar high while she deliberated. Colin was crowned the winner, to much applause! He'll judge the next challenge in June...stay tuned for "30 Minute Meals"! 


Iron Chef: Evoolution Edition - Round Two
Friday, June 07, 2013

Last night, we had our second round of Evoolution Iron Chef, with EJ (winner of the previous round) judging as the rest of us threw down! It was a rousing success, and with the Family Fued-esque theme of ‘Things you would find at a BBQ”, everyone brought their A-game. 


Mark made sandwiches with root beer-pulled pork, Alfoos Mango balsamic barbecue sauce, and Herbes de Provence and Alfoos Mango cabbage slaw. 



Rachel W. made Wild Mushroom & Sage rösti with prosciutto and Italian Aromatic salt, topped with portobello mushroom and Blackberry Ginger pork tenderloin. 



Teagan made potato salad with Thai Lemongrass Mint and Porcini mayonnaise, pickles, paprika and boiled eggs.


Rachel P. made sandwiches with Harissa mayonnaise, garlic and scallion pork meatballs and Blenheim Apricot-pickled carrot and radish slaw.



Curtis made stuffed peppers with homemade tomato sauce, garlic, Italian seasoning, rice and Basil olive oil, topped with parmesan cheese and an 18-Year Traditional reduction.



Azkaa toasted challah from Dauphine with Butter olive oil and Strawberry balsamic, then topped it with crushed strawberry-mascarpone cream, fresh strawberries, a dusting of icing sugar and sprigs of fresh thyme. 



After plating and pictures, we all dug in, EJ focusing on the task at hand while the rest of us chatted away. Most of us came hungry in preparation, so by the time we cleared the plates, EJ was ready to announce Rachel P. (a.k.a. me, behind the scenes in the above photo) as the winner. 

As I tend to do the blogging on these nights, we've decided that the lovely runner-up, Azkaa, will be the judge for the next round. Prepare for "Pickles, Jams, Chutneys and Relishes"! 

Iron Chef: Evoolution Edition, Round One
Sunday, March 24, 2013
“What can I do with this?” is one of the most common questions we get from our customers; of course, everyone knows that our oils and vinegars make a delicious salad dressing or marinade, but here at Evoolution, we’re always striving to discover new uses for our products.

To that end, Curtis and Rachel W. decided to make things interesting at our last staff meeting with a little friendly competition. After each of us drew an oil and a vinegar from a basket, we were charged with creating a dish using the combination. Needless to say, some of us were luckier than others, but the wheels were turning in everyone’s heads regardless.

Finally, on one evening last week, it was showtime!

Mark was fortunate enough to be nominated as the judge:



We started with dessert first…

Azkaa made Eureka Lemon ice cream with a Serrano Honey reduction.



Rachel P. made a Wild Blueberry Balsamic crumble with a Persian Lime topping and burnt-honey mascarpone cream.



EJ made seared rare tuna with a Pomegranate, ginger and soy reduction, served with Roasted French Walnut edamame purée, wasabi cream and spiced, candied peanuts.



Rachel W. made Tuscan Herb crostini topped with Vermont Maple Balsamic onion jam, beef tenderloin and horseradish cream.



Curtis made BBQ Ribs with Organic12-Year Balsamic, Garlic EVOO and Applewood Smoked Salt.



After much tasting, and with careful attention to his criteria, Mark named the winning dish: EJ’s tuna! EJ will be the judge for the next competition, and the rest of us have a couple of months to work with our new combinations before the big showdown.
