The fresher the better
Thursday, October 24, 2013

We've got a whole new crop of varietals in from the southern hemisphere, including our first oil from South Africa! Most of the oils were crushed at the beginning of the summer, making them some of the freshest olive oils around. The freshness of olive oil is crucial - the fresher the oil, the better it tastes and the better it is for you. 

NPR recently did a piece on freshness in olive oil, citing Tom Mueller (author of Extra Virginity) among others. 

The Olive Oil Times has some tips on how to keep olive oil fresh.

Tom Mueller has a guide on how to buy great olive oil.
